# Content Workflow

## Purpose
Turn ideas into published, trackable, strategically aligned assets.

## End-to-End Flow
1. Research or ideation creates a candidate concept.
2. Idea is stored or selected in Ideas.
3. Approved concept becomes a Content Engine item.
4. Slide copy or script is developed.
5. Design brief is created.
6. Production tasks are created in Tasks.
7. Asset is reviewed by QA/editorial.
8. Asset is posted manually or through the chosen publishing path.
9. Results are reviewed and learnings are recorded.

## Stage Definitions

### Stage 1: Idea selection
Questions:
- What funnel stage is this for?
- What business goal does it serve?
- Is it aligned with the masterclass and course promise?

### Stage 2: Content brief
Required fields:
- title
- funnel stage
- objective
- audience
- hook
- core idea
- CTA
- format
- platform

### Stage 3: Copy development
Output should include:
- slide-by-slide copy or post structure
- caption draft
- CTA language

### Stage 4: Design brief
Output should include:
- format specs
- visual tone
- template family
- text hierarchy guidance
- any visual references or constraints

### Stage 5: Task creation
Minimum tasks for most assets:
- writing or refinement
- design or production
- QA or review
- publishing

### Stage 6: QA
Check:
- funnel alignment
- brand tone
- readability
- CTA quality
- platform fit

### Stage 7: Publishing and review
After publishing:
- update status in Content Engine
- capture results in Metrics where appropriate
- note what angle was tested and what happened

## Operating Rule
No asset should jump straight from idea to design without a clear content brief and funnel-stage classification.
